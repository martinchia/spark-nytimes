Donald J. Trump drew back Friday from calling for a national database to keep track of Muslims in America, saying that the suggestion had come from a reporter. Mr. Trump caused a stir Thursday night when he was asked by an NBC reporter if all Muslims should have to register with the federal government and replied: “They have to be.”  When pressed on how such a database would work, he said that people would have to sign up in different places and that it would be an issue of management. On Friday, he took to Twitter to distance himself from the idea. I didn't suggest a database-a reporter did. We must defeat Islamic terrorism & have surveillance, including a watch list, to protect America — Donald J. Trump (@realDonaldTrump) November 20, 2015  Mr. Trump did not issue a full retraction, maintaining that surveillance and a “watch list” were important measures for keeping America safe. And his spokeswoman said on Fox Business News that the Republican presidential candidate would not rule anything out when it comes to fighting terrorism. Republicans and Democrats criticized Mr. Trump on Friday for threatening to violate the religious freedom of Muslims, but some conservative commentators such as Rush Limbaugh defended him and said that the news media had put words in his mouth.
