 An article on Tuesday about the devastation of forests from pine bark beetles in the West misstated the year and precise location of a new infestation in the Canadian province of Alberta. A freak wind event blew beetles into Alberta in 2006, not 2007. And the beetles were blown into northern Alberta, where they had never before been observed. (The beetles were already part of the ecosystem in southwestern Alberta.) A version of this correction appears in print on , on Page A4 of the New York edition with the headline: Corrections.
