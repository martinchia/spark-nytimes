 The “Really?” column on Tuesday, about the debate over the risk of breast cancer from eating grapefruit, misstated the relationship between the enzyme CYP 3A4 and grapefruit. Grapefruit contains several compounds that inhibit the 3A4 enzyme, thus increasing the level of substances that are normally metabolized by 3A4, including estrogen. Grapefruit does not contain the enzyme itself. A version of this correction appears in print on , on Page A4 of the New York edition with the headline: CORRECTIONS: FOR THE RECORD.
