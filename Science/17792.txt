 To the Editor: Re “Empathy’s Natural, but Nurturing It Helps” (Personal Health, Feb. 16): Jane Brody writes, “The absence of empathy fosters antisocial behavior, cold-blooded murder, genocide.” Empathy is a necessary condition for a civilized society, but not enough. How one uses the knowledge about another gained via empathy, is equally important. One could say that those who employ psychological torture on prisoners have empathy for them and are extremely attuned to what will make them talk.
