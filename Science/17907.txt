 An article on Tuesday about the surface of the oceans misstated the depth of the chemically distinct layer of water at the surface, which scientists call the microlayer. It is usually considered to be 60 microns, about one five-hundredth of an inch, or thinner than most human hairs — not one-hundredth of an inch.
