 To the Editor: Re “Finding a Scapegoat When Epidemics Strike” (Essay, Sept. 1): Donald G. McNeil Jr. quotes Dr. Martin J. Blaser as to why Jews were largely spared during the Black Plague — because at Passover time grain was removed from their houses discouraging rats that spread the disease. While this may have been true, another possible reason is that one of the commandments for Jews is to wash their hands before eating bread at a meal. This simple centuries-old tradition that continues to this very day, may be the best way to stop the spread of epidemics such as swine flu even in our modern times.
