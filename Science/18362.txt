 To the Editor: In “A Doctor in Iraq, Watching a Faith Healer at Work” (Cases, Dec. 28), Dr. Amir Afkhami makes the welcome point that healers who empathically engage with their patients’ suffering can often be more successful than doctors with degrees. Until we agree that much psychological distress is symptomatic of the social illnesses of misogyny, racism and poverty, we miss the point and reproduce the problem. Jane A. Hassinger Ann Arbor, Mich.
