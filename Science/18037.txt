 To the Editor: “A Fight to Win the Future: Computers vs. Humans” (Essay, Feb. 15) notes that “over the past century and a half the shift from being a largely agrarian society to one in which less than 1 percent of the United States labor force is in agriculture is frequently cited as evidence of the economy’s ability to reinvent itself.” My late colleague and Nobel economics laureate Wassily Leontief used to say that this reassuring paradigm should also be considered from the point of view of the dray horse — for which there was never a comeback.   Sylvain Cappell New York The writer is a professor of mathematics at New York University. Science Times welcomes letters from readers.
