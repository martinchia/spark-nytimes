 To the Editor: “The Nicotine Blast” (Q&A, Sept. 16) is an excellent reminder about nicotine’s effects. But one cannot help noticing that the list of effects,  which “relate to heart attacks, cardiovascular accidents, or strokes,” is duplicated by the effects of caffeine. Yet caffeine is being resuscitated as a brain stimulant, and its consumption is greatly increased by the huge portions served these days. C. M.
