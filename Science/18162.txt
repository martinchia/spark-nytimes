 To the Editor: Re “Hypertension: In Retreat, but Hardly Vanquished” (Personal Health, April 22): As Jane E. Brody observes, a good way to control this malady is with pharmaceuticals, but it is not the only way. Some years ago, I did not like my readings (140/90 and higher). I made three changes: I stopped eating lunch (I am a strict vegetarian); I eliminated all extraneous salt; I increased my exercise program. My blood pressure dropped so dramatically that at one visit I asked the nurse if I was still alive. Robert HauptmanColchester, Vt. Science Times welcomes letters from readers.
