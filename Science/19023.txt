THE NEW YORK TIMES

APRIL 14, 2014
 Researchers have discovered a tiny new species of crayfish in Australia. Weighing just over seven grams, the Gramastacus lacus is one of the world’s smallest freshwater crayfishes, and is distinguished by a large male genital papilla and raised ridges above the eyes. Described in the open-access journal ZooKeys, it resides in swampy areas on the eastern coast of Australia from Wamberal Lagoon to Wallis Lake, where it periodically burrows into the water table to survive dry periods, effectively hiding from scientists seeking to study it.
