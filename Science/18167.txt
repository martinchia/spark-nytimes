 To the Editor: Dr. Elissa Ely’s patient in “In Delusions of Romance, Genuine Comfort” (Cases, May 13) insisted that men burrowed into her apartment, through her “chest” and ate her up from the inside. That is a psychotic description of metastatic lung cancer. Perhaps she had her diagnosis right from the start, and perhaps the name for her experience — cancer — gave her relief and allowed her to love. Surely the unfairness in treating psychosis is knowing when to listen to patients and how. Gail SinaiNew York Science Times welcomes letters from readers.
