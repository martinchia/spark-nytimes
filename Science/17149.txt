 To the Editor: Re “Contraception and the Courts” (News Analysis, April 9): One of the ironies of making emergency contraception available over the counter is that it will not affect the out-of-pocket cost. Like other OTC drugs, Plan B is not covered by health insurance. The Affordable Care Act won’t change this. According to federal guidelines, “Contraceptive methods that are generally available OTC are only included if the method is both F.D.A.-approved and prescribed for a woman by her health care provider.” Given the pending lawsuits against these guidelines, it’s not clear if even prescription contraceptives will be covered. Removing the age restriction on Plan B may make it easier to find, but it won’t make it more affordable. Heather Munro Prescott New Britain, Conn.
