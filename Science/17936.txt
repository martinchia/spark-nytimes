 To the Editor: Re “Returning to Classrooms, and to Severe Headaches” (Well, Aug. 31): As a longtime pediatrician, I have found that sleep deprivation is almost always the primary cause of migraine in children. When I can coax parents to get their children a healthy amount of sleep, the headaches often go away. But many parents refuse to accept this simple explanation or are unable to make their children’s sleep needs a priority when planning family schedules.    Will Wilkoff, M.D. Brunswick, Me. The writer is the author of “Is My Child Overtired?”  A version of this letter appears in print on September 7, 2010, on Page D7 of the New York edition with the headline: Headaches And Bedtime.
