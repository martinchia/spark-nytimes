Blockbuster announced this week that it had accepted a preliminary bid to sell itself — for $290 million, as part of a plan to more quickly exit bankruptcy. The chain also plans to shutter 609 stores. It’s a bit of an ignoble state of affairs for what once was the dominant name in home movie watching. But fewer and fewer customers have chosen to make any night a Blockbuster night, thanks to the rise of the online movie rental service Netflix.
