Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati said on Monday that it had added 10 new partners to its practice.  The law firm, best known for its prominent Silicon Valley client list, announced promotions in Austin, Tex., San Diego, Calif., New York, San Francisco, Washington and at its headquarters in Palo Alto, Calif., where it added five partners. “We take great pride in the legal skills, expertise, and commitment of our associates, as well as their immense dedication to clients,” Steven E. Bochner, Wilson Sonsini’s chief executive, said in a statement. The 50-year-old firm is adding to its upper ranks during a particularly busy time for technology deal-making and the initial public offerings market. It also comes as many of its clients, like Google, face increasing antitrust scrutiny from regulators. The firm tapped William B. Chandler, former chancellor of the Delaware Court of Chancery, to join as partner in May. Below is a list of the new partners-elect: Joseph M. Alcorta, Corporate & Securities. Based in Austin, Alcorta represents public and emerging growth companies, investment banks, venture capital firms, and private investors in a wide variety of corporate and securities law matters.
