Wall Street traders, who typically receive the fattest year-end bonuses among bank employees, are poised to suffer the biggest pay cuts as revenue at their divisions have dropped an average of 12 percent this year, Bloomberg News reported. Goldman Sachs, which makes most of its money from trading and set a Wall Street pay record in 2007, cut average compensation 26 percent in the first nine months. By contrast, Bank of America, which is based in Charlotte, N.C., and employs branch managers and brokers as well as bankers and traders, raised average pay 10 percent.
