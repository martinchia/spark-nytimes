As Donald J. Trump began to solidify his path to the presidency on Tuesday night, international markets fell sharply. Wall Street was expected to react similarly, but was up on Wednesday.
Related Article
 Throughout the presidential race, financial indicators had moved as the odds changed in favor of one candidate or the other. In recent days, news favoring Hillary Clinton’s campaign was accompanied by surges in stock prices, while news favoring Mr. Trump’s campaign coincided with reverse swings.             Because of concerns in Mexico about a Trump presidency, the value of the peso against the dollar dropped precipitously. In the United States, the value of the dollar fell against other currencies, like the euro, the pound and the yen.       Source: Reuters Advertisement
