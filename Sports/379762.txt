Advertisement  CROSS-COUNTRY  O'Sullivan Beats Favor for Title Sonia O'Sullivan raced to a 75-yard victory over Wisconsin's Suzy Favor and led Villanova to the women's team title in the National Collegiate Athletic Association championships yesterday in Knoxville, Tenn. O'Sullivan, a junior from Ireland, covered the 5,000-meter course in 16 minutes 6 seconds as the Wildcats scored 82 points to win the event for the second successive year. Favor, an eight-time N.C.A.A. track champion, placed second in 16:18. Providence was runner-up with 172 points in the 22-team field. The men's 10,000-meter race was captured by Jonah Koech of Iowa State. Koech, a Kenyan, ran 29:05. Arkansas scored 68 points to defeat Iowa State, the defending champion, for the men's team title. N.B.A. Sixers Hold On to Defeat Hornets Charles Barkley and Hersey Hawkins each scored 31 points and the Philadelphia 76ers held off a late Charlotte rally to beat the Hornets, 109-101, last night in Philadelphia. (AP) Lakers 122, Nuggets 105: James Worthy scored 25 points and Magic Johnson had his third triple-double of the season as visiting Los Angeles held run-and-gun Denver to its lowest point total of the season. The Nuggets, who had been averaging 132.2 points a game, lost for the ninth time in 10 games. Their previous low was 110 points against Dallas on Nov. 3. Johnson had 21 points, 11 assists and 10 rebounds. (AP) Bucks 114, Jazz 104: Jack Sikma scored 9 points to spark a third-quarter spurt as Milwaukee rallied from a 17-point deficit to beat visiting Utah.
