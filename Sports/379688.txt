A postgame news conference took a dramatic turn after the Montreal Alouettes’ victory in the Grey Cup on Sunday night.   Quarterback  Anthony Calvillo led the Alouettes to a  21-18 victory,  Montreal’s second straight win over the Saskatchewan Roughriders in the Grey Cup, then surprised reporters with an announcement.  Ed Tait of The Winnipeg Free Press: EDMONTON — He is a champion. He is a future hall of famer. He is the leader of a Montreal Alouettes team that can now be included among the greatest dynasties in Canadian Football League history. And now this shocking revelation: Quarterback Anthony Calvillo will need surgery to remove a lesion in his throat that was discovered in August but kept secret until now.  Last year’s Grey Cup finish between the two teams will always be remembered for a spectacular gaffe. The Saskatchewan players celebrated a championship after a last-second field-goal miss by Montreal, but the Roughriders  were flagged for too many men on the field and the Alouettes were given another chance ….
