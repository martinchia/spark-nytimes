Advertisement  He rewrote the books in the National Basketball Association last season as the league's leading scorer, most valuable player and top defensive player, so it was not too surprising yesterday that the Chicago Bulls rewrote Michael Jordan's contract. The revised one will pay him $25 million over the next eight years. Jerry Krause, the general manager, said: ''Michael is among the highest-paid players in the history of professional basketball.'' Krause said the pact restructured portions of Jordan's current contract and extended the agreement. Jordan said he was satisfied. ''It's something you take as a compliment,'' said Jordan, who is entering his fifth pro season. ''I'm happy.'' (AP) We are continually improving the quality of our text archives.
