Advertisement  TENNIS  Edberg Begins Quest  Top-seeded Stefan Edberg, who is seeking to regain the world's No. 1 ranking he lost to Jim Courier, beat Sergi Bruguera of Spain, 6-4, 4-6, 6-4, yesterday in the opening round of the Donnay Indoor Championship in Brussels. Patrick McEnroe also advanced by defeating Andrei Cherkasov, 6-4, 7-6 (7-4). Boris Becker, seeded third, needed only 66 minutes to defeat Jonas Svenson of Sweden, 6-4, 6-0. (AP) Washington Advances in Memphis: MaliVai Washington used a powerful serve to defeat Bryan Shelton, 6-0, 6-3, yesterday in the second round of the Federal Express International tournament in Memphis. Washington, No. 14, was the first seeded player of the $780,000 indoor tournament to begin singles play. Fifteenth-seeded Cristiano Caratti of Italy was upset by Grant Connell of Canada, 7-6 (7-4), 7-6 (8-6), in a later match. (AP) TRACK AND FIELD  The Vaulting Bubkas  The Bubka brothers finished first and second in the pole vault at the Chitose International Indoor meet yesterday in Osaka, Japan. Sergei Bubka won with a vault of 19 feet 4 1/4 inches, but he made three unsuccessful attempts to clear 20-1 1/4, a quarter inch over his world indoor record.
