Advertisement  BOB JOHNSON , who coached Pittsburgh to the Stanley Cup in May, was named to coach the United States' Canada Cup team yesterday by USA Hockey. Johnson coached the last three United States Canada Cup teams, in 1981, 1984 and 1987. He also coached the 1976 United States Olympic team and coached the United States National Team four times. Johnson's boss with the Penguins, General Manager CRAIG PATRICK , will serve as co-general manager of the American team, which will be made up entirely of National Hockey League players.
