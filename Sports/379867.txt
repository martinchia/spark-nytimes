Advertisement By THE ASSOCIATED PRESSMAY 26, 2014
 Adam Scott made a 7-foot birdie putt on the third hole of a playoff Sunday to end his first week as the world’s No. 1 player with a victory at the Crowne Plaza Invitational in Fort Worth. Jason Dufner, who made a 25-foot birdie putt on No. 18 in regulation, slid a 40-footer past the hole when he and Scott played the 18th for the second time during the playoff. Scott then made the 7-footer for his 11th PGA Tour victory. Scott and Dufner each shot four-under-par 66 to finish at nine under, the highest winning score at Colonial since 1999. ■ Rory McIlroy came from seven shots back at the start of the final round to shoot a six-under 66 and win by one stroke with an overall 14-under 274 at the European Tour’s BMW PGA Championship in Virginia Water, England. Ireland’s Shane Lowry birdied the final hole for a 68 and finished second at 13 under. ■ Jessica Korda won the Airbus L.P.G.A. Classic for her second victory of the year, birdieing four of the final five holes to break out of a tight pack in Mobile, Ala. Korda made a breaking 15-foot birdie putt on the par-4 18th for a seven-under 65 and a one-stroke victory over Anna Nordqvist. Korda finished at 20-under 268. ■ Colin Montgomerie won the Senior P.G.A. Championship in Benton Harbor, Mich., finishing with a six-under 65 for a four-stroke victory over Tom Watson.
