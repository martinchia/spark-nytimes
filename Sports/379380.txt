 The Washington Wizards reached the playoffs for the first time since 2008, clinching an Eastern Conference berth Wednesday night with a 118-92 win over the visiting Boston Celtics. Marcin Gortat scored 22 points to lead the Wizards, who gathered for a celebratory huddle at midcourt after the final whistle. John Wall, the 2010 No. 1 overall draft pick, added 13 points and 10 assists. The Wizards’ playoff position had been relatively secure for several weeks, but the milestone was worth celebrating for a franchise whose win totals since its last postseason appearance have been 19, 26, 23, 20 and 29 — never finishing better than 24 games below .500. Jared Sullinger had 25 points, and Rajon Rondo had 13 to lead the Celtics. BULLS 105, HAWKS 92 D. J. Augustin scored 23 points, and visiting Chicago held off slumping Atlanta, bumping the Hawks out of the final playoff spot in the Eastern Conference with two weeks left in the regular season. Chicago took control with a 16-3 run in the second quarter and led the rest of the way. The Hawks lost for the 21st time in 27 games, this one dropping them slightly behind the Knicks for the eighth playoff seed.
