Advertisement  MALIK SEALY of St. John's has been named the top college player in the New York metropolitan area for the second year in a row. Sealy, who averaged 22.6 points a game, was named winner of the Lieut. Frank J. Haggerty Award, which is given by the National Invitation Tournament and the Metropolitan Basketball Writers Association to the best player from 64 colleges in the New York-New Jersey-Connecticut area. STEVE LAPPAS, who led Manhattan (25-9) to the most victories in school history and has since accepted the coaching job at Villanova, received the Peter A.
