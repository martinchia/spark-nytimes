Advertisement  N.B.A.  Bullets 161, Nuggets 133: Bernard King scored a season-high 52 points -- including 23 in Washington's 48-point first quarter -- at Landover, Md., last night. King had 30 points by halftime to help Washington take an 81-61 lead, then added 10 in the third quarter as the Bullets stretched their advantage to 117-88. Denver lost for the seventh time in eight games. King, the N.B.A.'s third-leading scorer, made 20 of 35 shots and was 12 for 14 at the free-throw line. His 52 points was the most by a Bullet since the franchise moved from Baltimore after the 1972-73 season, surpassing a 50-point effort by Moses Malone in April 1987. King also had eight rebounds, six assists and a steal. He was removed with 3 minutes 54 seconds left after coming up 4 points short of the franchise record of 56 points, set by Earl Monroe in 1968. (AP) Jazz 131, Clippers 102: Karl Malone scored 31 points yesterday afternoon in Salt Lake City and Jeff Malone added 29, including 16 in the third quarter, as the Utah Jazz won for the 11th time in 14 games. John Stockton scored 16 points and had 13 assists for the Jazz, who went two games behind the idle San Antonio Spurs in the Midwest Division. (AP) Trail Blazers 120, Cavaliers 114: Clyde Drexler scored 20 of his 28 points in the second half and banked in a 3-pointer with 19.7 seconds left to help Portland reach 27-3, at Richfield, Ohio, last night. The victory was Portland's fifth in a row and eighth in nine games, and it also was the 100th victory for Coach Rick Adelman. Cleveland has lost 4 in a row and 10 of 11. (AP) Pistons 99, Rockets 84: Joe Dumars scored 20 of his season-high 30 points in the first half for the Pistons at Auburn Hills, Mich., last night. Detroit won its third in a row, holding the Rockets to 36 percent shooting and limiting Akeem Olajuwon to 17 points and 11 rebounds. Otis Thorpe had 17 points and 12 rebounds for Houston. Isiah Thomas had 14 assists and 13 points for the Pistons. (AP) Hornets 109, Magic 100: The Hornets ended a losing streak at 11 games. The two teams had come in with a combined 19 losses in 20 games. Maple Leafs 6, Penguins 3: Mike Krushelnyski scored two goals to help the Maple Leafs cool off the league's hottest team last night in Toronto. The Penguins, who had won eight of their nine previous games and led the league with an average of 4.2 goals a game, got two from Jaromir Jagr and one from Kevin Stevens. (AP) Horse Racing  Winning in the Fog: In fog so thick racegoers couldn't see the horses they had bet on, Chas'Whim captured the $100,000 Congressional Handicap at Laurel (Md.) Race Course yesterday. Chas'Whim, who was the 119-pound high weight among a field of seven, completed a mile and a quarter in 2:01 4/5 over a muddy track under the jockey Allen Stacy. Jet Stream finished a length and three-quarters back in second place and Reputed Testamony was another three-quarters of a length behind in third. Chas'Whim earned $60,000 for his owners-breeders, Elmer and Harriet Heubeck of Ocala, Fla. The 3-year-old gelding returned $9.20 for $2 to win. (AP) Tennis  Seles Leads Yugoslavia: Monica Seles overcame jet lag yesterday to spark Yugoslavia to a 3-0 victory over Italy and a place in the quarterfinals of the Hopman Cup team championship at the Burswood Superdome in Perth, Australia. Seles, who had arrived less than 24 hours earlier, defeated Raffaella Reggi, 6-2, 6-7, 6-4, in the opening singles match to give fifth-seeded Yugoslavia a 1-0 lead. Goran Prpic of Yugoslavia beat Paolo Cane, 6-4, 6-3, in the second singles match, and Seles and Prpic then combined to beat Cane and Reggi, 8-3, in mixed doubles, which was reduced to one set because Yugoslavia had clinched the victory. The Yugoslavs will meet the Soviet Union, with Andrei Chesnokov and Natalya Zvereva, in the quarterfinals today. In other quarterfinal action, the top-seeded United States will play Germany.
