Advertisement By FILIP BONDY With the National Hockey League facing a lengthy crisis, striking players last night met to debate whether they would form a rival league in the fall. "That's one of the options we're considering," Mark Messier, captain of the Rangers, said. "In 1972, the World Hockey Association was formed in just two months. We have the resources and the people to do it." Messier said the players would find somewhere to play hockey, if they were still on strike in September or facing the possibility of a lockout by owners. "There will be places for us," he said. "If we have to start up our own league, then we will." Wayne Gretzky of the Los Angeles Kings, however, was more skeptical on that front. "It takes years of preparation just to run a tournament like the Canada Cup," Gretzky said. "I don't think it would be that easy." Advertisement Nontheless, player representatives discussed that issue and others in a private session last night, before reporting about breaking events to their membership. John Vanbiesbrouck said his Ranger teammates would be hurt by the news, but were in solidarity with the decisions. "We are most bitter, about not being able to play," Vanbiesbrouck said. "But this is not a confrontation between the New York Rangers players and Rangers ownership. This is between the Players Association and ownership."
