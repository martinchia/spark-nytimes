Advertisement  When the San Diego Padres named REGGIE WALLER as their director of scouting, they got more than a sharp talent evaluator who had spent 10 years in the Houston and Seattle organizations, most recently as assistant to the Mariners' vice president for baseball operations. They also got their first black executive. To the 36-year-old Waller, being first in baseball is important, but not for breaking the executive color line. "The only thing I care about in baseball and being first," Waller said, "is coming in first in the World Series." (AP) We are continually improving the quality of our text archives.
