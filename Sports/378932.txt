Advertisement By GERALD ESKENAZI As they prepare for the 1992 season, the Mets and Yankees appear to have baseball's best clean-up hitters. According to a formula devised by JOHN DEWAN and DON ZMINDA , who put out the book "Stats 1992 Baseball Scoreboard." A clean-up hitter's job is to produce extra-base hits that drive in runs. Thus, Dewan and Zminda rate clean-up hitters on the basis of slugging percentage. In the previous 10 years, using the Dewan-Zminda formula, the erstwhile Bronx Bombers never had a player in the top 10. But leading the list now is DANNY TARTABULL (only a quibbler would point out that he played for Kansas City last season). The Mets have the No. 2 man in old, reliable HOWARD JOHNSON . The book (Stats Publishing) can be ordered by calling 1-800-63-STATS.
