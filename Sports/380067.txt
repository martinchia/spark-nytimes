Advertisement By THE ASSOCIATED PRES Maple Leafs 4, Panthers 3 The Toronto Maple Leafs last night became the third National Hockey League team to start the season with eight consecutive victories via a 4-3 decision over the expansion Florida Panthers on Rob Pearson's goal 2 minutes 17 seconds into overtime in Miami. "We knew how tough it was going to be," said Toronto's captain, Wendel Clark, who helped set up the winning goal and finished with two assists. The Leafs can set the N.H.L. record tomorrow night at Tampa Bay. The record was set by the 1934-35 Leafs and matched by Buffalo in 1975-76. Toronto won on Pearson's second goal, a rebound he put behind John Vanbiesbrouck. Blackhawks 3, Nordiques 2 Advertisement Jeremy Roenick's power-play goal with 56 seconds left gave Chicago a home victory over Quebec that snapped a three-game losing streak. Michel Goulet, on a pass from Roenick, tied the score at 2-2 early in the final period. Red Wings 6, Jets 2 Keith Primeau had two goals and an assist before picking up a game misconduct, leading Detroit to a home victory over Winnipeg. Stars 6, Senators 5 Advertisement Brent Gilchrist sparked a four-goal comeback, then scored the winner in overtime as Dallas shocked the Senators in Ottawa. Gilchrist, whose screened shot at 10:01 of the third period made it 5-2, put a rebound behind Ottawa goalie Craig Billington 1:56 into the overtime.
