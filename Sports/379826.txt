Advertisement  AP Luc Robitaille scored twice as the Los Angeles Kings rallied for four third-period goals tonight to beat the Calgary Flames, 5-3, in the opener of their Smythe Division playoff series. Calgary led, 3-1, early in the third period before the Kings, playing without Wayne Gretzky, scored three goals in just over six minutes. Todd Elik and Tony Granato both scored on power plays and Steve Kasper added an empty-net goal as Los Angeles took a 1-0 lead over the defending Stanley Cup champions in the four-of-seven series. Gretzky, receiving treatments in Los Angeles for a back injury, missed his first playoff game in 10 years. Advertisement The Kings were leading, 1-0, when Mike Krushelnyski threw his stick in an attempt to block a shot by Al MacInnis with 3 minutes 8 seconds left in the first period. Referee Kerry Fraser called for the penalty shot, the 20th in Stanley Cup history and the first for a Flame. MacInnis broke in, waited for Kelly Hrudey to go down, then shot the puck over him.
