Advertisement  AP Arnold Palmer coasted to a two-under-par 70 today to win his first PGA Senior Tour event since 1985 with a four-stroke victory in the $325,000 Crestar Classic. ''My goal was to shoot under par on the back nine,'' said Palmer, the fifth wire-to-wire winner on the Tour this year. ''I just did what I set for myself but I hoped it would be better.'' Palmer, 59 years old, finished with a 54-hole total of 13-under 203. He started the day with a three-stroke lead, birdied the second and third holes and had only one minor challenge for the $48,750 top prize that lifted his earnings this year to $115,366. He fired a one-under 35 on the back nine. Larry Mowry, the defending champion,tied for second at 207 with Jim Ferree, who shot a 69, and Lee Elder, who had a 70.
