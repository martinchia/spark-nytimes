Advertisement  GOLF Kartzmann Gains, Beating Cash Ninth-seeded Mark Kartzmann of Australia survived a tie breaker in the second set to defeat Pat Cash, also of Australia, 6-4, 6-7, 6-4, in the first round of the Japan Open in Tokyo yesterday. Amos Mansdorf of Israel, seeded 10th, and Milan Srejber of Czechoslovakia, 13th, also advanced to the second round. Mansdorf beat Chris Garner of Bay Shore, L.I., 6-2, 6-2. Srejber beat Kentaro Masuda of Japan, 6-2, 6-4. (AP) HORSE RACING Lorenzoni Edges Eternal Flight Lorenzoni held off favored Eternal Flight in the stretch yesterday to post a 1 1/4-length victory in the $31,000 Mr. Prospector Purse at Aqueduct. The victory was Lorenzoni's first in four starts this year. Eternal Flight, with Chris Antley up, was second in the field of six 3-year-olds and up, 1 3/4 lengths ahead of Clef. Lorenzoni, ridden by Laurent Goossens, covered seven furlongs in 1:22 2/5 on a fast track to earn $18,600. He returned $15.40 for $2 to win.(AP) YACHTING Whitbread Leaders Reach Florida The three leaders of the Whitbread round-the-world race arrived safely in port in heavy rain and wind in Fort Lauderdale, Fla., yesterday with the final transAtlantic leg ahead. Steinlager 2 of New Zealand crossed the line at 3:41 A.M. The 84-foot ketch took 22 days 16 hours to complete the 5,475-mile leg from Uruguay, and arrived 34 minutes ahead of Fisher & Paykel. The British sloop Rothmans came in third at 8:30 A.M.
