Advertisement By WILLIAM N. WALLACE The Barcelona Dragons continue to be    the    success story of the World Football League, which has not had too many others. The Dragons, now on a road trip in the United States, continue to be adored in their home city in Spain, where they have broken their attendance record twice this season with crowds of 40,875 and 49,675. The Dragons have also sustained the football lives of their general manager, JACK TEELE ; their coach, JACK BICKNELL, and their most popular player, ERIC NAPOSKI. Teele, for 30 years a National Football League executive with the Rams and the Chargers, said recently: "I've never had so much fun. It's like the old days in the N.F.L., the 1960's. At Barcelona, I've limed the field, helped set the goal posts, done everything." Bicknell, for 10 years the Boston College head coach, said, "Best experience of my life." The Catalans have taken to Naposki, an outside linebacker from Yonkers and the first one to have a fan club, La Penya Naposki. Naposki played briefly in the N.F.L., mostly with the Patriots.
