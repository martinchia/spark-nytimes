Advertisement  He is a .269 hitter who averages 3.9 yards a carry, and after winding up his latest minor league stint with the Tidewater Mets, outfielder/running back D. J. DOZIER is apparently ready to return to the Minnesota Vikings. At his agent's request, Dozier was removed from the lineup for Tidewater's final International League game last night, according to the Mets' director of baseball operations, GERRY HUNSICKER , who said Dozier did not want to risk an injury that might impede his footall career. But whether Dozier, who joined the Vikings in 1987, will find a warm welcome in Minnesota -- or anywhere else in the National Football League -- remains to be seen. The Vikings, who obtained HERSCHEL WALKER from Dallas in 1989, now use a one-running-back offense and have a wealth of backup backs. Team officials said yesterday that they had not been able to trade Dozier, who is not under contract, and had given him permission to approach other teams.
