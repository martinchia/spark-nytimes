Advertisement  Four Americans -- MICHAEL JOHNSON, MIKE POWELL, RENALDO NEHEMIAH and SANDRA FARMER-PATRICK -- are contending for the overall titles in the I.A.A.F. Mobil Grand Prix outdoor track and field circuit. Two meets remain on the 19-meet schedule, the Van Damme Memorial in Brussels, tomorrow, and the Sept. 20 Grand Prix final in Barcelona, Spain. Among the men, Johnson, a sprinter, Powell, a long jumper, and Nehemiah, a high hurdler, are in a five-way tie for second place with 45 Grand Prix points each after the Grand Prix meet Tuesday in Berlin. NOUREDDINE MORCELI, a miler from Algeria, and JAN ZELEZNY, a javelin thrower from Czechoslovakia, are also tied for second. They trail the world record-holder in the pole vault, SERGEI BUBKA of the Soviet Union, who has 51 points. (AP) We are continually improving the quality of our text archives.
