Advertisement  AP The California Angels confirmed today that they plan to make the city of Tempe their spring-training headquarters for at least 15 years starting in 1993. At a news conference near city-owned Diablo Stadium, officials of the team and the city credited a new state ball park-renovation law with the Angels' decision to leave Palm Springs, Calif. The legislation was passed June 19 and signed into law last week. It gives Maricopa County, the center of the so-called Cactus League with six teams, and Yuma and Pima Counties, which have one each, taxing authority to raise money dedicated to improving baseball training complexes.
